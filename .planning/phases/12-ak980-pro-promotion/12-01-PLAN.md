<objective>
Deliver the KEYBOARD-04 honesty-critical wireless RGB rate-limiter for `ak980pro`
(`0c45:8009`): add an `isWireless` capability hint to `DeviceDescriptor`, register
`ak980pro` with `isWireless = true`, and route every RGB write in `ProprietaryKeyboard`
through a `writeRgb` funnel that enforces a `<=10 writes/sec` (>=100ms inter-write spacing)
rate-limit ONLY when the device is wireless. The throttle is opt-in per-device, NOT a global
throttle (per KEYBOARD-04 + REQUIREMENTS Out-of-Scope row "Per-key RGB writes at 60+ keys
without rate-limiting on wireless link").

Purpose: This is the ONE genuinely build-testable deliverable of Phase 12 (no hardware
needed). The wireless dongle can lose keystrokes when an RGB transition floods its queue
(Pitfall 24); the rate-limiter is the mitigation. By injecting a fake clock into the device
under test (alongside the existing `MockTransport` DI seam), the >=100ms spacing invariant is
asserted deterministically against captured `writes()` timestamps without touching real
hardware or sleeping the test thread.

Output: an `isWireless` descriptor field, a `writeRgb` rate-limit funnel, and a
MockTransport + injectable-clock unit test that pins the wireless-throttled / wired-unthrottled
behaviour.
